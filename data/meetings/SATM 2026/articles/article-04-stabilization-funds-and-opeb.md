## Warrant Text

General Stabilization To see what sum of money the Town will vote to raise and appropriate, transfer from available funds or otherwise provide for the purpose of supplementing the Stabilization Fund established under Article 22 of the warrant for Annual Town Meeting of 1961, as authorized by Chapter 40, Section 5B of the General Laws, as amended; Operational Stabilization To see what sum of money the Town will vote to raise and appropriate, transfer from available funds or otherwise provide for the purpose of supplementing the Operational Stabilization Fund established by vote of the 2011 Spring Annual Town Meeting under Article 4, as authorized by Chapter 40, Section 5B of the General Laws, as amended; Capital Stabilization To see what sum of money the Town will vote to raise and appropriate, transfer from available funds or otherwise provide for the purpose of supplementing the Capital Stabilization Fund established by vote of the 2010 Fall Annual Town Meeting under Article 2, as authorized by Chapter 40, Section 5B of the General Laws, as amended; OPEB To see what sum of money the Town will vote to raise and appropriate, transfer from available funds or otherwise provide for, the Other Post-Employment Bene fits Liability Trust Fund established by vote of the 2017 Spring Annual Town Meeting under Article 15, pursuant to the provisions of Chapter 32B, Section 20 of the General Laws as amended by section 15 of Chapter 218 of the Acts of 2016; or otherwise act thereon.

Discussion Highlights:

Ari Sky, Deputy Town Administrator/Finance Director of the Town of Natick presented the article. He shared that there was some redistribution of Free Cash and Capital projects, so the bottom line has not changed from earlier drafts. A list of the current funds and balances was also shared: 55 A member asked about the FAR and I&I funds, and referenced that those are truly restricted funds. He asked if we invest these into the Mass Municipal Depository Trust and if there was interest. Mr. Sky confirmed they are invested and there is interest. The member then asked the projected gross liability for OPEB Trust. Mr. Sky said the most recent study said about $151 million. Per the town’s financial management principles and policies, the strategy has been to satisfy the pension liability and then once complete in 2030-31 move towards the OPEB liability. A member asked the presenter to give...

Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- OPEB is for Non Retiree Health Benefits.
- The town is currently at a $2,000,000+ deficit in the previously allocated snow removal budget; there are different ways to address the funding gap - through some re- allocations within the Department of Public Works budget, and if needed, a portion of Free Cash.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- He shared that there was some redistribution of Free Cash and Capital projects, so the bottom line has not changed from earlier drafts.
- Errickson added that we have not yet met the pre-COVID balances of many of these accounts, though we are on target (primarily Capital and General stabilization are not yet back to pre pandemic levels) A member asked about the cost of snow removal and whether any of these funds would go to that.
- Sky said they do not anticipate needing to use these funds.
