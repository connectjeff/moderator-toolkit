## Finance Committee

Recommendation: Favorable
Vote: 9-1-3
Unanimous: not unanimous
Date voted: March 24, 2026
Motion vote threshold: Requires a Majority Vote

Discussion Highlights:

Joshua Ostroff presented Article 18. Mr. Ostroff presented this article as both a celebration of the 250th anniversary of the 1776 Natick Resolve for Independence, a Natick Town Meeting resolution supporting the then-pending declaration of independence from England, and a reaffirmation of our of current commitment to the United States Constitution. Further, the article intends to positively affirm the indigenous peoples of the land currently comprising the United States and Natick, to counter the derogatory and negative references to such peoples included in both the declaration of independence and the US Constitution. 117 This article was reviewed and discussed at two separate FinCom meetings. At the initial meeting, certain FinCom members pointed to language in an earlier version of the motion that pointed to our current political climate in a way that such members felt would be...

Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- Ostroff presented this article as both a celebration of the 250th anniversary of the 1776 Natick Resolve for Independence, a Natick Town Meeting resolution supporting the then-pending declaration of independence from England, and a reaffirmation of our of current commitment to the United States Constitution.
- A FinCom member asked if the sponsor would be willing further amend the motion to remove language stating that Town Meeting “opposes the curtailment of rights embodied in the United States Constitution and its amendments that Town Meeting members have taken an oath to support and uphold” also on the basis of such language potentially being divisive.
- Niki Lefebrvre, Ph.D., Executive Director of the Natick Historical Society (which has the original 1776 Natick Resolve for Independence in its collection and has the full text of the resolution available on its website) expressed enthusiastic support for Article 18.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- It was noted that although it was unanimously adopted the original 1776 Natick Resolve for Independence was likely divisive.
- Another member of the public and a FinCom member each noted that both the Declaration of Independence and the US Constitution include disparaging references to indigenous peoples.
- The sponsor noted that countering such disparaging references are specifically why Article 18 includes language naming and proclaiming respect for such indigenous peoples.
