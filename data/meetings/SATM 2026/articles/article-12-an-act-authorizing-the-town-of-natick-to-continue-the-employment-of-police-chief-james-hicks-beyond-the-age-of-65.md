## Warrant Text

To see if the Town will vote to authorize the Select Board to file Special Legislation with the General Court under the Home Rule Amendment to the Massachusetts Constitution or take any other action thereon, said Special Legislation to read as follows; provided, however, that the General Court may make clerical or editorial changes of form only to the bill, unless the Select Board approves amendments thereto before enactment by the General Court, which amendments shall be within the public purposes of said petition; or otherwise act thereon. AN ACT AUTHORIZING THE TOWN OF NATICK TO CONTINUE THE EMPLOYMENT OF POLICE CHIEF JAMES HICKS BEYOND THE AGE OF 65. SECTION I. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, James Hicks, Police Chief of the Town of Natick, may continue to serve in that position until reaching age 67, the date of his retirement, or the date he is relieved of his duties by the Select Board at their discretion, whichever occurs first; provided, however, that he is mentally and physically capable of performing the duties of Police Chief. The Select Board may, at the Town's own expense, require that James Hicks be examined annually by a physician designated by the Select Board, to determine such physical and mental capability to perform the duties of his oﬃce. No further deductions shall be made from the regular compensation of James Hicks, pursuant to chapter 32 of the General Laws for service subsequent to his reaching age 65; and upon retirement for superannuation, he shall receive a superannuation retirement allowance equal to the allowance that he would have been entitled had he retired upon reaching age 65

Discussion Highlights:

The article was presented in the Finance Committee Hearing on March 12 by Bruce Evans of the Select Board and Chief James Hicks. The police chief and the deputy chief are subject to mandatory retirement statutes this year. For each of them to continue in his respective role into 2027 will require successful passage of enabling home rule petition legislation by the state legislature. Article 12 is the article to enable the home rule petition for the chief’s continued employment. It was stated that the average police force tenure is not historically high and that the supervisory experience on the force is generally light, especially with the imminent retirements of two lieutenants and one sergeant. Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- It was stated that tight staffing currently for rank and file officers makes it difficult to promote officers needed for street duty.
- A Finance Committee member noted that among the points made in support when Town Meeting acted on the Deputy Chief position was that it could facilitate succession planning, and yet the current configuration does not facilitate succession without the home rule petitions.
- The presenters responded that while the intention of the Deputy Chief position included succession planning, that intention depended on the applicant pool for the deputy position supporting the intention.
