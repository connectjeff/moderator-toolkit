Amendment	to	the	Main	Motion	under	Article	18	 
O#ered by Frank Foss, Precinct 10 
I	move	that	the	town	vote	to	amend	the	main	motion	by	replacing	it	in	its	entirety	and	inserting	the	following	in	its	place:		Town	Meeting	commemorates	the	250th	anniversary	of	the	Natick	Town	Meeting	Resolve	for	Independence,	originally	approved	on	June	20,	1776;		And	that	Town	Meeting	affirms	its	support	of	the	Constitution	of	the	United	States	through	the	following	resolution:		Whereas:		1.	The	Natick	Town	Meeting	Resolve	for	Independence,	enacted	June	20,	1776,	reflects	the	courageous	spirit	and	determination	of	the	land’s	inhabitants	and	our	ancestors	in	the	pursuit	of	liberty	and	justice	in	the	face	of	tyranny.				2.	The	principles	articulated	in	the	1776	Resolve	resonate	deeply	in	our	contemporary	society,	reminding	us	of	the	long-standing	importance	of	local	governance	and	the	right	of	self-determination.		3.	The	Constitution	of	the	United	States	serves	as	a	guiding	document	that	enshrines	the	freedoms	fought	for	by	our	forebears,	ensuring	the	protection	of	rights	for	all	citizens.		Now,	therefore,	be	it	resolved	that:		1.	We,	the	inhabitants	of	Natick,	do	hereby	celebrate	the	legacy	of	the	Natick	Town	Meeting	Resolve	for	Independence	as	a	testament	to	our	local	history	and	our	ongoing	commitment	to	liberty.			2.	We	affirm	our	support	for	the	Constitution	of	the	United	States,	and	through	our	oath	of	office	recognize	it	as	a	vital	framework	for	justice,	equality,	and	the	rule	of	law.		3.	We	encourage	the	citizens	of	Natick	to	reflect	on	the	significance	of	this	anniversary	and	to	engage	in	discussions	that	promote	civic	responsibility	and	historical	awareness.		4.	We	instruct	the	Town	Clerk	to	place	a	copy	of	this	resolution,	the	Natick	Town	Meeting	Resolve	of	Independence	dated	June	20,	1776,	and	the	Constitution	of	the	United	States	in	the	town	records	so	that	such	are	available	for	viewing	by	the	wider	community	to	honor	this	important	milestone.
