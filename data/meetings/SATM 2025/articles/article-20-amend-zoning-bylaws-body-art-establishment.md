## Finance Committee

Recommendation: Favorable
Vote: 12-0-0
Unanimous: unanimously favorable
Date voted: March 20, 2025
Motion vote threshold: Requires a ⅔ Vote

Discussion Highlights:

CED Director Loomis presented Article 20, and explained that the goal of this was to update the definition of Body Art to align it with the Board of Health’s definition. The Board of Health is the primary regulator of Body Art establishments, and this article seeks to align with its definitions. This also expands the areas where Body Art establishments are allowed - from a limited area within the Golden Triangle. A citizen petition recently expanded it into Natick Center. At that time, there were questions 90 about why we were not allowing this in more locations. This article expands Body Art establishments into commercial and industrial districts. A member asked why a special permit was required for this use in certain places. Director Loomis said the Planning Board thought a lot about this and tried to essentially align this with the Personal Services use and where that is allowed....
