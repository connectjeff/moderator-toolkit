Frequently Asked Questions
Metro West CD, founded in 1991, is a regional non-profit community
development corporation with a track record of completing successful
affordable housing developments. We have developed 205 units of
affordable housing with an additional 75 units in the construction pipeline,
including 5 Auburn Street. Visit metrowestcd.org for more information.
Natick's growing need for affordable housing is driven by high demand,
rising costs, and a severely limited inventory, making it increasingly difficult
for many residents to find and maintain affordable homes. Natick’s Housing
Production Plan notes that nearly one-third of renters and homeowners are
considered housing cost-burdened, paying more than 30% of their income
on housing expenses. According to Housing Navigator MA, of the
approximately 1,500 Subsidized Housing Inventory (SHI) units in Natick, only
approximately 837 units are affordable apartments (the rest are market
rate). Of these 837 affordable apartments, 541 are age restricted, leaving
only 296 apartments for other individuals and families. With market
vacancy at just 1%, this demonstrates an extremely limited supply.

Metro West CD is requesting CPA funds now for 2 reasons: 1) financial
need due to the presence of significant hazardous materials in the Eliot
School, design changes during permitting, and changes to Low Income
Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) equity pricing; and 2) timing - a commitment of
CPA funds now helps make our “OneStop” application for state funds far
more competitive and increases chances of a funding award. If the project
doesn’t receive funding from the state it could result in a 6-12 month delay,
increasing risk of deterioration of the Eliot School as it remains vacant.
While the project needs a CPC commitment now, MWCD would not be
looking to receive the committed funds until construction start or once
construction has begun.
CPA funds will be used for the general costs of construction for the
rehabilitation of the historic Eliot School into nine (9) apartments and the
new construction of twenty-three (23) apartments. 
CPA funds would make up just 3% of the estimated total project budget,
however the impact would be meaningful. For every $1 of CPA funding
provided, the project would be able to access approximately $30 in other
funds to support the production of 32 units of 100% affordable housing to
serve the Natick community. Other Metro West CD developments have
received CPA funds that contribute between 3% and 16% to project budgets.
