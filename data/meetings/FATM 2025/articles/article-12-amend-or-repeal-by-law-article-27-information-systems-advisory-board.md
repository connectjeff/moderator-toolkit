Discussion Highlights:

Richard Sidney, Select Board member, presented this article. He explained that this is part of a review of all the boards and committees that the Select Board appoints. As part of that effort, they realized that the Cable Advisory Board (CAB) and the Information Systems Advisory Board (ISAB) have been co-appointed for at least the last 8 years. The Select Board wants to adjust the charge and description of a combined board. Because the Information Systems Advisory Board is in by-law, re-writing that charge is difficult and requires Town Meeting action to ratify. The Select Board wants to keep a combined board in existence, but they want to take it out of the by-law. Multiple Finance Committee members expressed various levels of concern about this proposal. Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- Motions and Debate: Motions were made and seconded to: 1- recommend Favorable Action, and 2 - Refer the article back to the Sponsor.
- Supporters of Favorable Action argued that there wasn’t a downside to removing the ISAB from the bylaws, and that it makes the process of modifying the charge and composition of the Board much simpler.
- Supporters of Referral argued that Information Systems are critical and complex, and that weakening the requirement for having an advisory board is ill advised.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- Because the Information Systems Advisory Board is in by-law, re-writing that charge is difficult and requires Town Meeting action to ratify.
- Multiple Finance Committee members expressed various levels of concern about this proposal.
- Most of the concerns centered on the fact that boards and committees not specifically required in the bylaws can be sunset by the appointing authority without additional process or Town Meeting input.
