## Warrant Text

To see what actions the town will take to amend the Bylaws of the Town with regard to: Article 81 Chemical Storage Tanks and Systems of the ByLaws : To see what action the town will take regarding Article 11 Town Clerk of the ByLaws : 1) add “propane ”, “underground propane tanks ”, in Section 2a Applicability and elsewhere to provide for regulation of underground propane tanks and pads for above ground propane tanks to protect drainage areas, septic areas, wetlands and similar areas and 2) add exceptions for “propane ” in Sections 6(e), 9(c), 10(a) or other sections to provide that such propane tanks not be subject to fuel monitoring, inventory control and reporting as may be required for chemical tanks and other underground or above ground fuel storage tanks; Article 22 Town Counsel and New ByLaw Section 3) “i) Add a new Article to the ByLaws consistent with Article 2 Section 11 (e ) of the charter and any other relevant provision of the charter or general laws i) to establish a standing committee for and or of town meeting to study review and report on zoning bylaw changes in advance of town meeting action and to determine the composition, method of appointment, term, powers and duties of such committee and/or ii) amend Article 22 of the ByLaws to require and to provide for the appointment of legal counsel with specialized knowledge in zoning and land use matters, to provide for the method of appointment, qualifications, powers and duties of such counsel including but not limited to advising the representative town meeting, advisory committees of town meeting, the planning board and/or town moderator; o r o therwise a ct thereon .

## Finance Committee

Recommendation: A: Favorable
Vote: A: 13-0-0
Unanimous: unanimously favorable
Date voted: 9/16/2025
Motion vote threshold: Requires a Majority Vote

Discussion Highlights:

Motion A: Mr. Griesmer, Chair of the CBRC explained that last spring they were in front of the committee with a motion related to propane storage, but it had some issues that they had to fix so now they are bringing it back. This change would regulate the placement of such tanks underground by the Board of Health (BOH), which has an interest in regulating these to protect ground water etc. This change adds propane to the definition of “fuel” in the by-law that regulates underground fuel tanks to ensure that the BOH can regulate them. However, they don’t want to have propane tanks subject to the same regulations as oil and gasoline storage, so it is put in one section of the by-law but exempt from the rest of the by-law. A member asked what is being protected; is it about protecting against contamination from the fuel? Mr. Griesmer said that it is about protecting against...

Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- With something this important, we need to provide a standing resource to the town who can assist anyone who wants assistance with this including the Planning Board, the Finance Committee, and Town Meeting.
- Current counsel has multi-disciplinary legal staff, and it seems simpler to ask for additional legal support from within existing counsel.
- The intent of this bylaw change is to compel the hiring of additional zoning 164 specialty counsel, and to direct the administration to budget the additional appropriation needed.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- A member asked what is being protected; is it about protecting against contamination from the fuel?
- Griesmer said that it is about protecting against contamination in part but the purpose language doesn’t preclude the by-law accomplishing other goals.
- Another member explained that water that can unnaturally pond because of these underground tanks, which can lead to contamination even if the contamination is not from the propane in the tank itself.
