Discussion Highlights:

Ms. Loomis, Director of Community and Economic Development, presented this article. These changes are a requirement of the MA Attorney General for the Limited Site Plan Review bylaw, to clarify our compliance with the Dover Amendment. Members reviewed the AG’s letter to the Town on this matter, and questioned if the AG’s opinion was a directive that changes are necessary, or if only recommended. The Sponsor indicated that the AG is requiring the changes, and that Town Counsel concurred. Subsequent to the Finance Committee hearing, Town Counsel affirmed that opinion. Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- Members reviewed the AG’s letter to the Town on this matter, and questioned if the AG’s opinion was a directive that changes are necessary, or if only recommended.
- Motions & Debate: Motions were made and seconded for both a recommendation of Favorable Action, and for Referral to the Planning Board.
- Members supporting Favorable Action noted that it’s the opinion of the Planning Board, CED, and Town Counsel that these changes are required by the AG’s office, and make sense for full compliance with the Dover Amendment - which imposes limits on local review of certain projects brought forward by institution such as schools, religious organizations, qualified day cares, etc.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- Members supporting Favorable Action noted that it’s the opinion of the Planning Board, CED, and Town Counsel that these changes are required by the AG’s office, and make sense for full compliance with the Dover Amendment - which imposes limits on local review of certain projects brought forward by institution such as schools, religious organizations, qualified day cares, etc.
- A member also noted that the provisions related to day care had previously been in a different part of state law, and was unsure that they should be incorporated into these changes.
