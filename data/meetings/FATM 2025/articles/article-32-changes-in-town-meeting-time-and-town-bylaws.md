## Moderator Summary

Article 32 asks Town Meeting to act on `Changes in Town Meeting Time and Town ByLaws`, sponsored by Charter and ByLaw Review Committee. The official warrant text should be reviewed before this summary is finalized.

## Warrant Text

To see what action the Town will take to amend or to change: 1) Section 1 Rules of Procedure of Article 3 Procedure at Town Meetings of the Town ByLaws to change the title and authorship reference to “Town Meeting Time, A Handbook of Parliamentary Law, 4 th Edition ” (the 4 th Edition) or to a more or the most recent edition of Town Meeting Time updated by the Massachusetts Moderators Association; 2) Section 1 Rules of Procedure of Article 3 Procedure at Town Meetings to specify whether references to Town Meeting Time shall automatically refer to the most recent edition of Town Meeting Time or to provide that such versions, if any, subsequent to the 4 th Edition, must be specifically named and or adopted by Town Meeting vote; 3) Other sections or Articles in the Town Bylaws provided such amendments or changes are directly related or responsive to changes in the 4 th Edition that are i) identified, ii) noticed, iii) highlighted and/or contained within a 4 th Edition chapter or section that is listed in either a) the “Town Meeting Time 4 th Edition Appendix C: Mapping of 3rd Edition to 4th Edition Sections ” or b) the “Editorial Notes on Town Meeting Time 4 th Edition ” as such documents are published by the Massachusetts Moderators Association with a copy of each document placed on file with the Town Clerk of Natick; (For convenience only, copies of documents referred to in a) and b) above may also be found at https://massmoderators.org/tmt4/ under https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qm1aekuPTpyyCXs_c5EkyIk79UrKaQ1j/view and https://drive.google.com/file/d/13ZOaF7J0SVqogsdywFpODwXBdkXfTGUM/view as long as such links are maintained by the Massachusetts Moderators Association. For record copy for this warrant article, please see copy on file with Town Clerk of Natick.) For convenience only, a copy of the 4 th Edition and the 3 rd Edition of Town Meeting Time has been ordered and will be placed on file with the Town Clerk of Natick when the copy arrives. For further convenience only, copies of the 4 th Edition may be purchased from the Massachusetts Moderators Association ( https://mass moderators.org ). Also, for convenience only, previous editions of Town Meeting Time might be accessed , subject to availability, through the Minuteman Library Network. o r o therwise a ct thereon.

Discussion Highlights:

Paul Griesmer, Chair of the Charter and Bylaw Review Committee (CBRC), presented this article. He explained that our current bylaw references the current edition of “Town Meeting Time”, and there is a new, 4th edition out. The CBRC was asked in July by the Moderator to take a look at the new edition and compare relevant sections in the old version to the new version. The main areas where differences were noted: (1) the new version does not require the seconding of motions. It says if your practice was always to have motions seconded, you 169 should continue that, but if it isn’t seconded, the Moderator “may” allow a motion to die for lack of a second. To make it clear that Town Meeting does want to have motions seconded, the CBRC is proposing adding that to our bylaw. A second is a small but easy check to make sure we aren’t having to debate anything anyone wants to make a motion...

Draft Arguments For (reviewer-confirm):

- In summary, he supports all four motions.
- Munnich, moved that we recommend favorable action on the subject matter of Article 32, Motion A, as printed in the meeting materials.
- Munnich, moved that we recommend favorable action on the subject matter of Article 32, Motion B, as printed in the meeting materials.

Draft Arguments Against (reviewer-confirm):

- The main areas where differences were noted: (1) the new version does not require the seconding of motions.
- That is our current bylaw which was done against the backdrop of the old version which said that if any public hearing is not held, the Moderator should as a matter of procedure, not allow it.
- Finally, the 3rd edition says a Motion to Dissolve is not in order as long as any article is not disposed of.
